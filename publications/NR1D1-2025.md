## Context  
Mesenchymal tumors (sarcomas) are rare cancers that arise from connective tissues such as muscle, fat, or fibrous tissue. Diagnosing them is often challenging because they can mimic other types of tumors (eg, carcinomas).

Some of these tumors are associated with specific genetic alterations involving the *NR1D1* gene.

<br>

## What this article shows  
This article describes a case in an 85-year-old patient with a sternal mass initially thought to be a cyst on ultrasound.  

Combined histological, immunohistochemical, and RNA sequencing analyses revealed an aggressive tumor characterized by an **NR1D1::MAML3 fusion**.

<br>

## Why it matters  
This tumor shows misleading features:
- on imaging, it resembles a cyst  
- on histology, it resembles a carcinoma

Without molecular analysis, misdiagnosis is likely.

In addition, the disease progresses rapidly, with metastases developing within a year.

<br>

## Contribution to knowledge  
This study:
- expands the spectrum of *NR1D1*-related tumors  
- confirms their aggressive behavior  
- shows that accurate diagnosis of rare tumors requires integrating morphology and genetics.
